# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: BIM/GIS producers at JRE Consultants (Daniel and colleagues) who build indoor mapping data (IMDF, GDB) for JR East stations and need to check, share, and publish it. Secondary: client-side stakeholders and reviewers who open a dataset from a link, look around a floor, and leave comments — without owning GIS software or GIS skills. Both work bilingually; Japanese station data viewed by mixed JP/EN teams is the normal case, not the edge case.

## Product Purpose

Kiriko is a Forma/ACC-style web application for indoor GIS datasets: a shared place where IMDF and GDB data is viewed on an interactive map, reviewed with in-place comments, and published to shareable links and embeds. Success means dataset review moves out of email, Excel sheets, and file-passing — a producer uploads a snapshot, a reviewer opens the link, and the feedback conversation happens directly on the map.

## Positioning

Forma/ACC for indoor GIS — cloud-style collaboration (view, comment, publish) for IMDF/GDB indoor mapping data instead of BIM models.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. Quiet surfaces, data first, nothing shouting — the interface should feel like a dependable professional tool that a non-GIS stakeholder can trust and a data producer can rely on. Warm neutrals with a single indigo accent; the map is the loudest thing on screen.

## Anti-references

- Desktop GIS (QGIS/ArcGIS): toolbar forests, dockable panel mazes, settings-dense chrome that intimidates non-GIS reviewers.
- Generic SaaS dashboard: stat cards, gradient heroes, marketing-y chrome — the template look of admin dashboards.
- Heavy enterprise (Autodesk Construction Cloud's weight): deep nav trees, slow modals, permission bureaucracy — keep the collaboration model, not the heft.

## Design Principles

- The map is the product. Chrome floats above it, stays quiet, and never competes with the data.
- Legible to non-GIS reviewers. Anyone with a link can open a dataset, understand what they see, and leave a comment without training.
- Feedback lives on the map. Comments are pinned in place, with status, where the issue actually is — never in a detached list-first workflow.
- Precision builds trust. Attributes, warnings, and counts are shown exactly and honestly; the review workflow depends on the data being believable.
- Bilingual by design. Japanese and English are peers throughout — labels, data, and type rendering are designed for both from the start.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, full keyboard operability, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives. UI must hold up in both Japanese and English — line-height, truncation, and font stacks chosen with CJK text in mind.
